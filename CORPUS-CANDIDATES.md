# Corpus candidates

Candidate Project Gutenberg works for prebuilt `?corpus=` word-count tables
(issue: bundle famous, good literature beyond moby-dick). Ebook numbers verified
against PG's official catalog feed (`pg_catalog.csv`, 2026-07-19); where PG has
several editions of a work, the most-downloaded complete single-file edition was
chosen. Inclusion and ordering are curatorial judgment, not download rank.

**Edit me**: delete rows you don't want; annotate rows to change edition or
handling. Slugs will be derived from titles at build time.

Notes column flags: **play** = strip speaker headers (and offer the all-text
variant?); **verse** = line-broken poetry (fine for counting; watch for
translator/editor apparatus); **tr.** = translation into English; **archaic** =
pre-modern spellings will dominate the cloud.

## Handling notes (implementation phase)

- Boilerplate: existing `*** START/END ***` trimming holds, but many of these
  editions add transcriber notes, tables of contents, cast lists, and translator
  prefaces inside the markers — needs a per-book eyeball pass, plus generic
  strippers (illustration tags, footnote markers).
- Plays: strip `SPEAKER.` headers and stage directions; per the feature idea,
  optionally ship both `<slug>` and `<slug>-full` variants.
- Multi-volume works (Portrait of a Lady) need `make-corpus.py` to accept a list
  of ebook ids per slug.
- Bulk download should go through a PG mirror (MIRRORS.ALL) or the official
  feeds, not `www.gutenberg.org` page-scraping; keep the existing
  `~/.cache/words/gutenberg/` cache.
- Excluded on the quality criterion despite top-100 popularity: PG's perennial
  erotica/curiosa chart-toppers and ephemera (e.g. _The Romance of Lust_, _My
  Secret Life_, the CIA World Factbook).
